Local Space > World Space > Camera Space > Normalized Device Coordinates (NDC) Space > Screen Space
===================================================================================================
The point of rotation is at 0, 0, 0, which is often the center, or the bottom center of objects.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
To go from LS > WS, apply a Matrix to all vertices that make each verticy go from local > world coordinates
- Local to World Matrix

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
To go from WS > CS, multiply by world space vertices
Take the inverse of the camera matrix and apply it to the world coordinates
- Camera Matrix
Ex:
camera is at 100, 0, 10 in WS
object is at 100, 0, 20 in WS
the object is at 0, 0, 10 in CS

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
To go from CS > NDCS, Projection matrix, aspect ratio
- Projection Matrix
Aspect Ratio
FOV

- Scale/Flip Matrix to 2D coordinates
